# Product

## Register

product

## Users

TradFi-curious newcomers — people who understand index funds / ETFs and follow
market narratives (AI, defense, energy, politics) but find prediction markets
opaque and disconnected from "real" finance. They arrive wanting to understand
and act on a narrative, not to learn crypto plumbing. Primary context: desktop,
evaluating themes, comparing belief-market odds against the real equities of that
same narrative, then entering a curated basket in one signature — non-custodially,
into their own wallet. Secondary user: crypto-fluent power users who want the raw
odds, on-chain proof, and density underneath the clean surface.

## Product Purpose

Traditional Predictions is a non-custodial "index fund for prediction markets." It
packages each market narrative into a thematic **bucket** that fuses (a) real
prediction markets (Polymarket, on-chain) with (b) the tokenized real-world
securities of that same narrative — AI → NVDA and AI equities; Military/Conflict →
defense-contractor and oil/energy names. The hero is the **cross** between the two:
the "Sentiment Gap" — where belief-market odds sit versus where the related equity
sits within its published analyst bear/bull band. That signal exists nowhere else
because nobody else holds both sides of one narrative. Users browse themes, open a
bucket to see its prediction legs + related securities + the gap visualization, and
enter the curated basket in one signature. Success: a newcomer reads a narrative's
"belief vs. real-asset" picture at a glance and confidently enters a position.

## Brand Personality

Authoritative, lucid, precise. The voice of a great research desk: it states the
number and its source, and never hypes. Confidence comes from clarity and evidence
— live/verified tags, real on-chain tx hashes, analyst bands drawn on screen — not
from neon or persuasion. "Serious finance that happens to be about predictions."
Emotional goals: trust, legibility, the quiet competence of a pro terminal a
newcomer can still read.

## Anti-references

- NOT crypto-degen / neon-on-black / casino glow / hype.
- NOT generic AI SaaS: no cream/sand backgrounds, gradient text, uppercase eyebrow
  kickers above every section, or identical icon-card grids.
- NOT sterile legacy-bank dashboard: not gray, corporate-dead, or point-of-view-less.
- Approachability is welcome, but never gamified-Robinhood confetti or dumbed-down.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the cross, not a dashboard of parts.** The reason to exist is belief
   markets × the real equities of the same narrative. Every bucket leads with that
   relationship, never a generic price board.
2. **Evidence over assertion.** State each number with its source and freshness
   (live/fallback, on-chain tx, analyst band on screen). Never label the band
   percentile an "implied probability."
3. **Legible to a newcomer, deep for a pro.** Calm, clean default; density and raw
   on-chain truth one layer down. Gist in five seconds; drill to conditionIds/tx
   hashes on demand.
4. **Honest about what executes.** Never overstate what's on-chain. The prediction
   basket is the executable instrument; tokenized securities are the narrative's
   real assets — track/analyze, and offer access only where genuinely tradeable.
   Non-custodial; tap existing markets; never the venue.
5. **Precision is the aesthetic.** Tabular numerics, aligned grids, restrained color
   that carries meaning (belief vs. asset vs. state). The craft of a finance
   terminal is the brand.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA target. Colorblind-safe data encoding — belief vs. asset distinguishable
without relying on hue alone (shape/label/position, not just two colors). All motion
respects `prefers-reduced-motion`. Tabular figures, ≥4.5:1 body contrast for dense
data, keyboard-navigable browse and enter flows.
